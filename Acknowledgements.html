Acknowledgements

The HTML document type was designed initially at CERN in 1990 for the World-Wide Web project. The DTD was written, and the specification tightened up, by Dan Connolly in 1992.

An internet draft was release in June of 1992 after much discussion on the network and some enhancement in particular the addition of inline images introduced by the NCSA "Mosaic" software for WWW.

The FORMS material is derived from the HTML+ specification with the help of Dave Raggett.

This document is the work of many contributors. Many thanks to Erik Naggum and James Clark for making SGML technology available, and to Terry Allen, Dave Raggett, Marc Andressen, William Perry, and the rest of the WWW community.
