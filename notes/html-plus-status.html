HTML+ is a set of features and a DTD, originally proposed by Dave Ragget <dsr@hplb.hpl.hp.com> some time ago in response to feature requests from a wide variety of information producers and consumers with whom he consulted.

Parts of the proposal have been implemented in various WWW user agents: the FORM support in Mosaic and friends originates from HTML+. I believe Pei Wei's Viola implements much of HTML+ (somebody want to surf up a URL for Viola for me?). The emacs-based w3 browser by Bill Perry also implements much of the proposal.

Dave Ragget is now collaborating with some of the CERN folks on a browser called Arena that supports tables, math, and other HTML+ features.

Unfortunately, Dave Raggett doesn't seem to have a handy welcome page that always points to the most up-to-date copy of the HTML+ proposal -- he seems to beg, borrow, and steal space on HTTP/FTP servers now and again.

I recently asked Dave what the current status of all this is, and here's his response:
